Cartography and GIS

What the heck is a GIS?

I'm glad you asked! GIS stands for Geographic Information Systems, it is a discipline of geography. GIS manages, visualizes, and organizes geospatial data (data with longitude and latitude). There are two primary data types associated with GIS: vector and raster. The former consists of lines, points, and polygons. Raster data is pixelated, think of weather and NDVI maps.

GIS Maps

I created this map by downloading landsat (satellite) data from the US Geological Survey. It showcases the normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI). NDVI is a metric that describes vegetation health based on a plants ability to absorb red light and reflect infrared light. In ArcGIS Pro, I utilized the Raster Calculator tool to create a new NDVI layer with the equation: ("Infared" - "Red") / ("Infared" + "Red"). This equation will produce a result from -1 to 1 for a specific area. Finally I created a new layout and highlighted an area with high vegetative health/NDVI value. Asheville in comparison has weaker vegatative health. As a reminder this is raster data!

ModelBuilder

ArcGIS ModelBuilder is akin to the cool coding games you may have played in elementary. It is a visual way to run and reuse code. Typically a model you create is chained together like a snake. In this example I used summarize within to combine Chicago neighborhoods and census tracts into one augmented layer. Calculate fields is a valuable method to obtain statistical metrics with geospatial data. Learning how to utilize ModelBuilder is crucial to master spatial analysis in ArcGIS Pro. In addition Python and R are helpful to know for GIS work/scripting.

A Quick Note About Projections

You may or may not have noticed this glaring flaw...

I'll tell you whats wrong! Greenland is NOT larger than Africa! In fact, Greenland can fit into Africa 14 times! The reason why Greenland looks funky in this map is the projection I selected. A projection is the process of turning our 3D earth into a 2D plane. There is no such thing as a "perfect projection". Each has their own flaws, the cartographer should select the best projection possible in accordance with their primary message and audience. A lot of maps in our schools use the mercator projection. The mercator was created in the 19th century and emphasizes western nations over others (colonization). This gives many people the impression that west is larger and more significant than Africa, South America, and Asia. If you are making a map, research the best projection to use!!!!!
